# Risk IT: Understanding the Present and Designing the Future of Risk Prediction IT in Fire Departments

An NSF-funded research project investigating how fire departments identify, translate, and manage risks -- and how emerging data-intensive technologies can transform these practices in emergency services.

## About

Most U.S. cities are served by all-hazards fire departments whose mission has shifted dramatically: 911 calls for diverse medical cases continue to rise while budgets remain flat. Fire personnel rely on expert systems, organizational processes, and professional intuition to manage **risk work** -- the practices through which they assess, mitigate, and prevent potential harms. As data-intensive IT becomes widely available, these practices will evolve, but the sociotechnical tools and practices necessary to support this transformation have been slow to develop.

This project proceeds through three phases:
1. **Ethnographic Research** -- Documenting current risk work practices and data resources across multiple fire departments
2. **Co-Design** -- Collaboratively prototyping data-intensive tools and practices with fire department partners
3. **Broad Evaluation** -- Gathering feedback from emergency services personnel on how prototypes shift visions for the future
